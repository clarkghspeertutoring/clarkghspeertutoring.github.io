Peer Tutoring Reflection

General Comments

Throughout this peer tutoring journey, I developed both my mastery in music and interpersonal communication skills. Each session presented unique challenges and learning moments, helping me grow as both a tutor and a learner.

I am very very glad that a few of my peer tutoring students from last year (Math 10), including but not limited to Brianna, Ian, and Shannon, took initiative and became peer tutors themselves this year. (Shoutout to Brianna who was also peer tutoring in the music program! I hope she enjoyed working with the Grade 8s. I was about to teach the 8s, but the teachers decided to put me in block C, which is the 9 and 10s, whom are also wonderful groups of students)

Below are snapshots from this experience, some with a meaningful story behind it.

First Conducting Session

This photo captures the first time I led the whole class. I conducted the grade 9 concert band through a few scales and warmup songs. It was overall a wonderful experience, since it was my first chance to meet everyone in the band together.

Group Photo

I had a selfie taken with the Band 9 students after my conducting session; it was after then when many of these student starts to greet me in hallways and classrooms. Though I want to seek improvement from them in terms of music skills, I still primarily try to be nice and bond with them -- which was a great decision. Managing 70 students at once is exceptionally difficult. Along with the grade 10s, compared to last year, it is 4x of names I have to remember.

"Paperwork"

As a peer tutor in band, we don't spend all our time dealing with students. In fact, we don't get to work with many students for the majority of time. This picture shows all the band music that I helped organizing upstairs on the shelf. My knowledge of excel has contributed greatly in improving the efficiency of this work.

The Office

About 40% of my time being a peer tutor this semester was spent inside this little room, the music office. I do lots sort of stuff: Sorting and tracking parent consent forms; help the choir write warm ups; enter student grades from playing tests; keep track of student info; cleaning a teacher's chair for the chocolate stain from another peer tutor :(

Preconcert Get-ready

I took this photo while charging all the stand lights for our year end concert. We usually do it a day or two before the concert to ensure all the lights work well for the performing bands. This was taken place in the practice room, the room where I spent my other 40% in. And yes, the remaining 20% goes to the band room and the attic above the room, where I did my conducting and organizing of music.

The Practice Room

This is another prespective of the practice room, but without the piano. Usually, my 1 on 1 sessions are in here and I use the piano to help the students find notes. Something that I did in this room as well, was sorting all the items for the silent auction for our Jazz and Java Event this Spring. I spent over 15 hours just in this room, taking up my lunch and spare blocks as well to make sure everything is organized. After the aution, I was also in charge of sorting the unsold items, which took another few days to do.

Holistic View of the Band Room

This is a Bird's-eye view of our band room, taken from the attic. It shows how big(or small) the band room is. The students downstairs make up the grade 10 concert band, which has less numbers than the 9s, making the room look less crowded. Analogy figures of instruments with red background frams can be seen on the walls, but I doubt anyone actually looks at them

What's Upstairs

This is the attic, where students store their instruments (Except for the Tuba and percussion instruments since they are stored downstairs in the room). It isn't a big space but many other important things are kept up here - Band Music Scores, Cases... and drinks/snacks for parties/events.

1 on 1 PT

This photo was secretly taken during my instruction. This is Niki, my assigned student earlier this semester. He is new to the instrument and has his own was of understanding towards the score and fingering. I enjoyed working with him and now he is able to strive on the Baritone Saxophone. He also awarded for Outstanding Progress at our concert last week (June 12). I think that is not only an achievement for him, but also a great recognition to me.

Year-end Party

This photo of Niki and I is taken at our year-end music mixer; I am happy that Niki liked his peer tutor. Photo taken by Tiana, also my student in G9 Band, who happens to be Niki's girlfriend. He already has one but I still do not.

Music Teachers

It's Ms. Smith and Mr. Han. Two very supportive, professional, and kind teachers. Ms.Smith focused more on the well being of the band; she often likes me to play along. On the other hand, Mr. Han also prioritzed the experience for a peer tutors by being more hands-off. I had chances to conduct, to help more students, and to stay with the percussion for some time to help with the rhythm. They gave me many opportunities to improve and take lead as a peer tutor. I thank them for all their help and endorsement.
